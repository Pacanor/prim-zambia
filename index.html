What we do

Our Specialities

Research in Health

We conduct research in population health – (HIV/AIDS, Adolescent and Youth Health, Non-NON-Communicable Diseases WASH and Nutrition), Health systems Strengthening (Community Health systems, Sexual reproductive health, health care management and Health care Monitoring and Evaluation). PRIM-Zambia

Research in Education

Education is an integral aspect of PRIM-Zambia’s work in a bid to expand the frontiers of knowledge, for the overall development of inclusive education, improvement in quality learning outcomes, community engagement and policy formulation. The primary purpose of educational research is to expand opportunities for different groups (boys and girls and CWDs as well as adults) inclusive education and improve the existing body of knowledge by providing solutions to different problems in pedagogy while improving teaching and learning practices.

Research in Youth and Women Empowerment

At PRIM Zambia, differences in gender, age and abilities exacerbate different experiences when emergencies strikes. There for our research specialities this thematic area has focused on Gender-based violence, including rape and early and forced marriage, which often increases during crises and when protection mechanisms deteriorate, depriving women and girls the fundamental rights to live free from violence. We have also focused on both social cash and emergency cash transfer to vulnerable households, women and youth and girls livelihoods improvement as well as research on child protection, Norms, culture and practices that impact on human life.

Other Research

PRIM Zambia has participated in multiple research projects across several domains, including Social Work, Social, Medical and Cultural Anthropology, Public Health, Gender Studies, Medicine and Education.

Recent projects

Dec 2021 - Feb 2022

SHE SOARS Program

Care International

The project aims to increase the enjoyment of health-related rights on the part of extremely vulnerable and marginalized women and girls, particularly out-of-school adolescent girls between 10 and 19 years of age in Kenya, Uganda, and Zambia. The project will provide training and support for educators and health care providers to ensure that adolescents have access to comprehensive sexuality education (CSE) and will provide CSE directly through skills and training packages for youth groups in all three project countries.

SRHR-HIV Knows No Borders project

International Office for Migration

The main objective for this baseline study is to set the benchmarks for the Phase II of the KNB project among target population. This assignment will provide contextual information to inform the design of the project - SRHR-HIV Knows No Borders phase II interventions that are tailored to the target groups' needs as well as document and determine policy barriers to migrant AYP and SWs' SRHRs to inform targeted policy advocacy.

Nov 2020 - Feb 2021

Assessment on the Impact of COVID-19 on Ending Child Marriage

Plan International and partners

To establish the impact of COVID-19 on ending child marriage in reference to planned efforts stipulated in the SADC Model Law on Ending Child Marriage and protection of those already in marriage and the African Union (AU) Common Position on ending child marriage. Specifically, this assessment sought to generate evidence on the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on measures to end child marriage in Eastern and Southern Africa covering Sudan, Egypt, Kenya, Malawi, Uganda and Zambia. This assessment was also meant to inform the development or alignment of programmes and advocacy strategies.

More About Us

Shareholder Engagement

At PRIM-Zambia, stakeholders' engagement is most or key aspects of the process. PRIM considers those who are affected by a problem, served by a public program, or employed by an organization or provide services to primary actors as key player in each stage of services delivery. For example, participating stakeholders are involved in the initial identification of the problem to be studied; the design of the research process or methods; the collection, documentation, and analysis of data; and the implementation of new approaches that result from the insights, lessons, and findings that emerge from the research. To adequately engage stakeholders, we create awareness and improve the knowledge of individuals and communities about the topics and research project. In addition, we build the capacity of local data collectors who facilitate the acceptability and conducting our research in a culturally feet model. This dialogue with stakeholders makes PRIM-unique in doing researchers with strict adherence to accountability to primary actors and transparent in our actions.
